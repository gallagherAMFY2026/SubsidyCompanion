Here is a list of websites offering free, accessible news and information about government subsidies, grants, and funding for farmers managing livestock (especially cattle) and sustainable land use in New Zealand:

Official Government and Program Sites
Ministry for Primary Industries (MPI): The central hub for all NZ government funding, grant schemes, and rural support programs, including recent smart farming grants and sustainable land management initiatives.

Department of Conservation (DOC): Details about conservation funding and grants for private landowners and community groups focused on habitat protection and sustainable land use.

Recent Grant Announcements and Industry News
Ecosmart.nz: Coverage on the latest round of government-supported smart farming grants (IoT, solar, precision livestock monitoring) including eligibility and deadlines.

Seedling Investments via RFSI Forum: News on private and public funds targeting regenerative dairy farming, soil carbon sequestration, and investment in sustainable livestock management.

Beehive (NZ Government press releases): Updates on agricultural policy changes, climate support programs, and emissions schemes affecting livestock producers.

Sustainable Farming and Land Management
Sustainable Farming Fund (SFF) – MPI: Details and news about the evolution to Sustainable Food & Fibre Futures, supporting innovative, environmentally responsible farm projects.

SDGs Partnerships (United Nations): Reports on NZ government support for sustainable fisheries and land use initiatives.

Additional Policy Commentary and Reviews
DevelopmentAid.org: Reviews on New Zealand’s historical and ongoing approach to farm subsidies and their impact on land management practices.

Mongabay: Global perspective articles that mention New Zealand-sourced funding for sustainable land and forest protection.

These sites provide timely news, official updates, program guides, and commentary on NZ's farm-related funding initiatives, with specific resources for livestock managers and those interested in sustainable land practices.
